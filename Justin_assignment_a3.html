What is a penalty shootout?

A penalty shootout is a very common way to break a tie in soccer when needed. A penalty shootout is divided into two phases. In phase I, there are five rounds in total. In each round, a player from each team will have a one-on-one penalty kick to try to score against the other team's goalkeeper. A team is eliminated if it will have less score than the other team even if if score all the subsequent goals and the other team misses all of them. If no winner was produced from phase I, the shooutout goes to phase II. Phase II uses a sudden death method in which if one team scores and the other does not in a round, the scoring team wins. This process repeats until a winner is produced. (Rule of Penalty Shootout)
